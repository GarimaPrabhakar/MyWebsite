Community

From the beginning, I've been obsessed with the idea of a world where humans and the environment can live in a symbiotic relationship. I was lucky enough to be a part of the process that makes it happen. I volunteer at Mass Audubon, a statewide non-profit wildlife sanctuary.

For much of freshman and sophomore year, I have volunteered at Mass Audubon's Worcester sanctuary, Broad Meadow Brook. My friend and I were introduced to the volunteering experience at Mass Audubon over the summer. Since then, I spend much of my Saturday volunteering at Broad Meadow Brook as receptionist, and also help out in Mass Audubon's biggest events and fundraisers, including the Butterfly festival (image on left).

One of my favorite events at Mass Audubon was a gathering of several green energy and space-related non-profits, including the prestigious Aldrich Astronomical Society, to celebrate Robert Goddard's launch of his first liquid-fuel rocket in Auburn MA (image on right). Being somewhat of a space nerd, I was extremely excited to see the event. I was even fortunate enough to meet the president of the Aldrich Astronomical Society, Jim Zebrowski.
