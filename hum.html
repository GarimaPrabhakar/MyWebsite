Humanities!

This humanities class is about the most unorthodox humanities class you’ll take in High School. I also believe that it is the most engaging, inspiring, and in general, awesome English class I’ve had in my entire life. This year, we’re combining psychology, history, sociology, and literature into one year-long study into the question: “Who Am I?”. Right now, we’re in particular focusing on social hierarchies and tackling the difficult yet crucial problem of stereotype threat.

Humanities Satirical Essay

While we were studying our central question, we also stumbled upon a variety of persuasion techniques the media and the literature uses to convince people of their argument. Below is an essay that I wrote on the effectiveness of the rhetorical tool satire. I analyzed the three satires “Fist Bump: The Politics of Fear” by Barry Blitt, “So Are We Just Not Going to Tell People New Year’s Was a Month Ago” by Nicole Mullen, and “Have They Run out of Provinces Yet?” by Calvin Trillin in comparison to the epitome of satire, “A Modest Proposal” by Jonathan Swift for effectiveness of persuasion.
